How This Calculator Works

This calculator uses statistical methods to project training costs based on the performance data from the provided academic study. Here’s a breakdown of how it works and the assumptions it makes.

Data from the Report

The core performance metrics are from "A Comparative Study on the Effectiveness of Smart-Training Al™ Versus Traditional Training Methods".

  • STAI™ Training (Group A): Mean Score = 80.5%, Standard Deviation = 2.9%
  • Traditional Training (Group B): Mean Score = 59.4%, Standard Deviation = 7.1%

Core Calculation: Estimating Pass Rates

The calculator assumes test scores for each group follow a Normal Distribution (the "bell curve" in the graphs). Using the mean and standard deviation, it calculates the probability that an employee will score above your "Required Pass Score".

Calculating Retraining Costs

To meet your "Required Group Pass Rate", you may need to retrain some people. For example, if you need 95% of 1,000 people to pass (950 people), but a method only has a 50% pass rate, you will need to train 1,900 people in total (`950 / 0.50`) to hit your target. The model calculates the wage cost for these additional training sessions.
